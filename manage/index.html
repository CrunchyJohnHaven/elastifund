Manage

A public-safe preview of the operator surface, not the private control plane itself.

The real `/manage` surface should answer four questions fast: what your instance ran, what it changed, what is waiting for approval, and what is blocked. This public stub shows the jobs that surface will own without pretending it is an authenticated dashboard yet.

What the operator surface is supposed to make legible.

Run ledger

What ran, when, and with which evidence artifacts.

Every pass should write a small, reviewable record instead of leaving operators to reconstruct reality from shell history.

Approval queue

What needs a human before it can move.

Capital transitions, outbound commercial actions, and ambiguous deploy choices stay above the loop.

Deployment state

Bundle validation, service posture, and drift.

The surface should make it obvious when a service is active, when launch is still blocked, and which bundle checks are failing.

Change review

What improved, what regressed, and what remains unverified.

The point is not activity. The point is to keep validated improvements legible enough to trust.
