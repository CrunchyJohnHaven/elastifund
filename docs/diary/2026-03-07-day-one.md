## What Happened Today

We funded our Polymarket account with seed capital that is no longer published in the repo and turned on the live trading bot. Within the first two 5-minute scan cycles, the system identified 3 markets it wanted to trade — Morocco PM negotiations, Zelenskyy peace talks, and Yemen conflict escalation. It placed maker orders on all three.

Then we cancelled all of them.

**Why?** These markets don't resolve for days or weeks. Our money would be locked up in slow-moving political markets while faster opportunities pass by. We had built a velocity scoring system specifically to avoid this, but it revealed something we hadn't fully appreciated: on Polymarket, **zero non-crypto, non-sports markets resolve within 1 hour.** The categories where our AI has genuine forecasting edge (politics, weather, geopolitics) ALL resolve in 12 hours to months.

This is the fundamental structural problem of AI prediction market trading, and it's one that nobody talks about in the "just use GPT to predict things" discourse.

---

## The Speed vs. Edge Dilemma

Here's the problem, simply:

| Market Type | Resolution Speed | LLM Edge? | Fee Status |
|-------------|-----------------|-----------|------------|
| Crypto candles (5min/15min) | Minutes | None | 1.56% taker fee |
| Sports | Hours | None | 0.44% taker fee |
| Politics/Weather/Geopolitical | Days to months | Yes (71.2% backtest) | Zero fees |

The AI is good at predicting slow things. Fast markets don't need AI prediction — they need speed and flow analysis. So we need two completely different signal sources running in parallel:

1. **LLM Analyzer** for slow markets (politics, weather) — deployed and working
2. **Smart Wallet Flow Detector** for fast markets (crypto candles) — building now

This is a genuinely interesting architectural insight: an "agentic trading system" can't just be one agent. It needs to be a system of specialized agents, each tuned to a different market microstructure.

## What We Learned

**The most important insight today:** The agentic trading problem isn't primarily a forecasting problem. It's a market selection problem. You can have the best probability estimator in the world, but if the markets where it works resolve too slowly and the fast markets need a completely different approach, your brilliant forecaster sits idle while your capital is locked up.

This is why most AI trading demos are misleading — they show the AI making a prediction and getting it right, but they don't show the weeks of capital lockup, the opportunity cost, or the fact that fast-resolving markets don't need AI prediction at all.

**The solution we're building:** A multi-signal architecture where each signal source is optimized for a different market speed. The LLM analyzer handles slow markets (12h+). The wallet flow detector handles fast markets (5-15min). The cross-platform arb scanner handles arbitrage (any speed). And a confirmation layer boosts sizing when multiple signals agree.
