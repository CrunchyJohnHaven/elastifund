# Replit Homepage Vision Brief

Date: March 8, 2026
Target: `elastifund.co`
Use with: the latest homepage screenshot

## Objective

This is a homepage messaging update for `elastifund.co`, not a repo-wide rewrite.

Do not redesign the whole site. Keep the single-page structure. Change the hero and the first follow-on section so the page leads with the vision first and the proof second.

The audience priority for this pass is:

1. people who could become believers, backers, contributors, or operators
2. technically serious readers who need immediate proof that the project is real

This should feel ambitious and sharp without becoming a fundraising page.

## Current-State Diagnosis

The current homepage is technically credible but emotionally underpowered.

What it communicates well now:

- the system is real
- the architecture is serious
- there is actual research, testing, and infrastructure behind it

What it does not communicate early enough:

- why this is a beautiful idea worth joining
- that people can participate by forking, contributing, and running their own instance
- that the project gets stronger through shared learning in public
- that the money has a purpose because 20% of net trading profits go to veteran suicide prevention

Right now the page opens with a narrow frame: "the AI decides what to trade." That is true, but it is not the most powerful version of the story. The more compelling thesis is lower on the page: people band together to run agents that get better, earn money, and give back. That is the idea that should lead the site.

## Core Message

The homepage should make this point within five seconds:

**Elastifund is an open-source project where people can fork and run autonomous agents, improve them together in public, pursue real profits, and direct part of that upside toward a real mission.**

Participation should be framed as:

- fork the project
- contribute code, research, and autopsies
- run your own instance
- allocate your own capital to your own instance if you choose

Do not frame participation as wiring money into a central pooled product.

## Top-Of-Page Layout Guidance

Keep the page single-page and scrollable.

Make these structural changes:

1. Hero stays at the top, but the copy becomes the dominant visual element.
2. The current technical terminal/status card becomes a smaller proof card.
3. The new three-pillar support section sits directly under the hero.
4. After that, the site can return to the existing "how it works" and deeper technical sections.

The visual hierarchy should be:

1. thesis
2. participation
3. mission
4. proof
5. mechanics

Not the other way around.

## What To Move Up From The Current Lower Vision Section

Pull these ideas upward into the first screen and immediate follow-on section:

- collective intelligence beats any individual agent
- this is bigger than one trading bot
- people can participate by forking and running their own instance
- shared learning is part of the moat
- profit and charity belong in the same opening story

The later Vision section can still exist, but it should feel like reinforcement, not like the first time the visitor discovers the real thesis.

## Guardrails

Do not use:

- `invest with us now`
- `send capital to Elastifund`
- `guaranteed returns`
- `passive income`
- `fully self-improving with no humans`

Preferred language:

- `join`
- `back`
- `fork`
- `run`
- `contribute`
- `build`
- `allocate capital to your own instance`

Keep the autonomy framing honest:

- humans build the machine, constraints, and safety rails
- agents operate within those constraints
- the system improves through open research, iteration, and shared evidence

Keep the first paragraph light on jargon. Do not lead with terms like `LMSR`, `VPIN`, `ELSER`, or `meta-model`.

## Proof Constraints

Use only already-verified public proof points from the March 8, 2026 repo materials and the supplied screenshot.

Preferred proof points:

- `131 strategies tracked`
- `345 tests passing`
- `76 research dispatches`
- `Polymarket + Kalshi`
- `MIT licensed`
- `20% of net trading profits to veteran suicide prevention`

Avoid brittle or easy-to-age claims unless they are clearly confirmed in the screenshot being used for this pass.

Examples to avoid in the hero area unless freshly confirmed:

- live uptime
- precise VPS status
- temporary build-stage counts
- intraday cycle telemetry
- narrow performance claims

## Acceptance Checklist

The update is successful if all of the following are true:

- a new visitor understands the vision within five seconds
- the first screen answers why this matters before explaining how the bot works
- the first screen makes participation feel possible
- the charity commitment is part of the opening story, not a footer detail
- the next section still proves the project is real, technical, and inspectable
- the later Vision section feels like reinforcement, not discovery
- the tone is bold and ambitious without sounding scammy, sloppy, or legally careless

## Quick Test Prompts For Review

After the update, a layperson should be able to say:

> This is a project where people can run and improve money-making agents together, and part of the upside goes to charity.

After the update, a technical reader should be able to say:

> This is an open-source autonomous trading system with real proof behind it, not just a manifesto.

If those two reactions are not immediate, the rewrite did not go far enough.
