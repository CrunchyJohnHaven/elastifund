# HONESTY BOX COPY

## 5 Versions of Essential Disclosure

These blocks belong prominently on the homepage (not buried in T&Cs). Pick ONE based on your audience and brand voice. Each is ~50-100 words.

---

## Version A: Plain Direct Language

**"The Real Talk"**

We haven't made real money yet. We've tested our model on historical markets and it performed well in backtests. Now we're testing it with real trades on Polymarket. So far: 17 trades placed, $0 profit or loss (no markets have resolved). Backtests often overestimate how well strategies work in reality. We built safety limits because we know our model could be wrong. We're putting our own seed capital where our mouth is while we figure out if this works.

---

## Version B: Trust-Building (Emphasizes Transparency as Strength)

**"Why We're Honest About Our Uncertainty"**

Most trading strategies sound great on a spreadsheet. Ours is no exception. That's why we're doing this transparently: showing you our real numbers (17 trades, $0 realized P&L), explaining what backtests can and can't tell you, and being upfront about the risks. We built this in public because we believe credibility matters more than hype. Our safety systems are here because we know how things can go wrong. We're early-stage research, not a proven money machine.

---

## Version C: Family-Office / Professional Tone

**"Important Disclaimer on Current Status"**

This is a seed-stage research program in prediction market trading. Performance to date: limited (17 paper trades, no resolved outcomes). All projections are derived from historical backtests (2023-2024) and are not indicative of future results. We acknowledge material risks: backtests overestimate live performance, edge decay from competition, platform and regulatory uncertainty, and model overfitting. We maintain operational safeguards including position limits, max-loss cutoffs, and drawdown monitoring. Investors should treat this as early-stage research, not a validated investment strategy.

---

## Version D: "Dad Can Understand This"

**"Here's What You Actually Need to Know"**

If you're wondering if we've made money: not yet. We're testing an idea on real markets with real money ($68 so far). The idea looks good when we test it on old data, but old data is always easier than real trading. We built safeguards to stop if things go wrong. We're small, we're early, and we might fail. But we're putting our own money down because we believe in this enough to bet on it. If you're investing, understand you're betting on a team, not proven results.

---

## Version E: Technical but Restrained

**"Evidence Status Summary"**

**What we know**: Our model outperforms random guessing in 532 historical backtests (68.5% accuracy on 2024 holdout data). **What we don't know**: whether backtests predict live performance (2 cycles, 17 trades, $0 resolved P&L to date). **Key limitations**: we assume midpoint pricing (real spreads are 2-5%), constant win rates (they won't be), and historical category patterns persist (competitive markets move fast). **Mitigations**: we deployed 6 safety systems and are reinvesting ~$1,000/week to validate assumptions. This is proof-of-concept research.

## PLACEMENT & STYLING GUIDELINES

### On Homepage Hero Section
Place near the fold, after the headline but before major claims. Make it visually distinct—consider:
- Light gray background or border box
- Sans-serif font (different from body text)
- Icon: warning triangle or "ℹ" (information)
- Small subheading: "The Honest Parts" or "What You Should Know"

### Design Example
```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  Important                                      │
│  We haven't made real money yet. All our        │
│  projections come from backtests on historical  │
│  data. We're testing with real money now        │
│  (17 trades placed). Backtests usually          │
│  overestimate real performance.                 │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Sizing Recommendation
- **On desktop**: 300-400px wide box in 2nd-fold area
- **On mobile**: Full-width box after hero image
- **Font size**: 13-14px (readable but not dominating)
- **Contrast**: High contrast—should be readable at a glance

## TONE DO's AND DON'Ts

### DO:
- ✓ Use "we" language (makes team accountable)
- ✓ Specific numbers: "$68 deployed," not "early stage investment"
- ✓ Name specific gaps: "no slippage modeling," not "limitations exist"
- ✓ Acknowledge market forces: "edge decay," "competitive advantage erodes"
- ✓ Show you've thought about failure: "backtests overestimate live performance"

### DON'T:
- ✗ Use false certainty: "we know" → "we believe" or "evidence suggests"
- ✗ Hedge with qualifiers: "relatively novel" or "somewhat unproven" (just say "unproven")
- ✗ Bury important info: honesty box must be prominent
- ✗ Use jargon without explanation: "Brier score" → "forecast accuracy metric"
- ✗ Over-apologize: confidence is good; false certainty is bad

## TESTING HONESTY BOX COPY

Before publishing, ask:

**Test 1 - Skeptic Test**
Read it as someone who doesn't trust you. Does it sound defensive or genuine? (Should be genuine)

**Test 2 - Omission Test**
Is there anything material we're hiding? Should we add it? (Examples: "we haven't validated on 2025 data," "fees may increase," "platform could change")

**Test 3 - Confidence Calibration**
Do we sound overconfident or underconfident? (Should match "early stage research")

**Test 4 - Specificity Test**
Can someone point to the exact metrics we're claiming? (Should be able to)

**Test 5 - Mama Test**
Would we say this to our parents? (If not, rewrite)

## FINAL RECOMMENDATION

**For homepage launch, use Version B (Trust-Building).**

**Reason**: Converts a necessary admission of uncertainty into a brand strength. Says "we have nothing to hide" which is more credible than trying to oversell limited evidence. Appeals to both retail and institutional audiences.

**Fallback**: If your brand is more corporate/formal, use Version C.
**If you have retail-focused homepage**: Use Version D.
**If you're talking to quants/technicals**: Use Version E.
