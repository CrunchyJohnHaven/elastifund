**Headline:** "The Bet"

**Subheadline:** "Can we predict markets better by removing bias and being honest about uncertainty?"

## SECTION 3: THE EVIDENCE PREVIEW

**Section Goal:** Reader sees the headline number, understands what it means, and knows there are limits.

**Headline:** "What the backtest shows"

**Subheadline:** "68.5% win rate. +$276 P&L. On 532 markets. Over 20 years."

**Supporting Copy (~120 words):**
Here's what historical testing reveals: in a backtest across 532 markets from 2004-2024, the system correctly predicted direction 68.5% of the time. P&L: +$276. Is this proof the system works on future markets? No. Backtests are hindsight. Markets change. Competitors are building the same thing. The model was built on this data, so it could be overfitted. The prediction accuracy barely beats a coin flip on statistical tests.

But it's not random. There's something there. Worth testing. Worth building on. Not worth betting your retirement on.

Live trading (started recently): 17 trades entered, $0 realized P&L. Too early to conclude anything.

**Visual:**
- A simple horizontal bar chart showing: backtest win rate vs. 50% (coin flip) baseline, clearly labeled "Historical"

- Text overlay: "Historical backtest. Live results pending."

- Monte Carlo fan chart (move to Evidence page)

## SECTION 4: WHAT WE BUILT

**Section Goal:** Reader understands this is a real system, not vaporware. Concrete inventory of work.

**Headline:** "The System"

**Subheadline:** "Here's what six months of engineering looks like."

**Supporting Copy (~100 words):**
A probabilistic market prediction system with five core components: (1) Anti-anchoring module removes emotional and historical bias from predictions. (2) Calibration engine makes the model honest about what it doesn't know, using Platt scaling and cross-validation. (3) Position sizing uses a quarter-Kelly approach to avoid ruin. (4) Category routing sends different strategies to different asset classes. (5) Safety rails prevent catastrophic drawdowns. Plus: a paper trading setup on a VPS, a comprehensive backtest framework, and nine papers documenting the research. Not production-ready. Early-stage. Real.

## MOBILE CONSIDERATIONS

- Stack sections vertically
- Simplify visuals for small screens
- Keep headlines and subheadlines short enough to read at a glance
- Make all links/CTAs thumb-friendly (large touch targets)
- Don't hide critical information behind "more" buttons

---

## TONE VOICE ACROSS HOMEPAGE

- **Direct:** Say what you mean. No hedge language.
- **Humble:** We don't have all the answers.
- **Evidence-driven:** Cite the backtest, cite the limits.
- **No jargon:** Explain terms or don't use them.
- **Anti-hype:** No "we're disrupting finance" or "AI beats humans."
- **Honest:** Name what's unproven and uncertain.

---

## KEY DESIGN PRINCIPLES

1. **Text-first design:** The words matter more than the graphics
2. **No terminal porn:** Don't screenshot a Bloomberg terminal or dark-mode dashboard
3. **No fake urgency:** No countdown timers, no "limited seats," no FOMO copy
4. **No testimonials on homepage:** Credibility comes from evidence and honesty, not quotes
5. **Progressive disclosure:** Summary on homepage, depth on deeper pages
6. **Responsive:** Works beautifully on mobile, tablet, desktop
7. **Accessible:** Good color contrast, readable fonts, semantic HTML
